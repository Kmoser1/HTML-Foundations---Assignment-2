Formatting

Height

  • (1857): Paper sheets had a height of ~22.5 cm, corresponding to the length of the cylinder.
  • (1860): A phonautogram of vocal singing recorded in 1860 and documented in an annotated discography had a height of approximately 19.4 cm.

Width

  • (1857) Width ~52.5 to 53 cm, representing the cylinder's circumference.
  • (1860) Width of the sheets reduced to ~50 or 50.5 cm, possibly due to a change in the recording cylinder.

Trace Pitch

  • pitch of helical multiple-trace groove was ~1.12 cm
